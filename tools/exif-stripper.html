    How to Remove EXIF Metadata from a Photo

    1. Drop your image — drag and drop one or more JPEG or PNG photos onto the tool, or click the drop zone to browse your files. You can load up to 10 images at once for batch processing.
    2. Review the metadata — a table lists every detected EXIF field with human-readable labels. If your photo contains GPS coordinates, a warning banner appears with a direct map link so you can see exactly what location data is embedded. Use the field group checkboxes to choose which groups to remove: Location / GPS, Device Info, Timestamps, or Camera Settings.
    3. Strip and download — click Strip All & Download to remove every EXIF field, Strip Selected & Download to remove only the checked groups, or Remove GPS Only for a one-click privacy fix. For multiple files, use Strip All & Download as ZIP.

    All processing is done entirely in your browser. Your images are never uploaded to any server. JPEG files are stripped using a lossless binary splice — pixel data is never re-encoded, so there is zero quality loss.

    Privacy Note

    Modern smartphones automatically embed GPS coordinates, device model, serial number, and timestamps into every photo you take. When you share photos online — on social media, in emails, or in documents — this hidden data can reveal your home address, workplace, or travel history. This tool removes that data before you share.
