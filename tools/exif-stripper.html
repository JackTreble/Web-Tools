    How to Remove EXIF Data from Photos — Free, No Upload

    1. Drop your photo onto the tool above, or click to browse your files. You can load up to 10 JPEG or PNG images at once for batch processing.
    2. Review detected metadata. The tool displays every EXIF tag found in the image — including GPS coordinates (shown as a clickable map link), camera make and model, capture timestamp, ISO, aperture, and more. A warning banner highlights GPS data.
    3. Choose what to remove. Click Strip All & Download to remove every EXIF tag at once, or use the group checkboxes to selectively remove only Location, Device Info, Timestamps, or Camera Settings. The "Remove GPS Only" shortcut in the warning banner strips just the location data in one click.
    4. Download your clean image. For multiple files, use Strip All & Download as ZIP to receive a single archive containing all stripped images.

    Your images are processed entirely inside your browser. Nothing is uploaded to any server — not even a single byte of your photo data. This tool works offline after the first load.

    Why remove EXIF data?

    • Privacy: Photos taken on smartphones embed precise GPS coordinates by default. Sharing a photo without stripping EXIF can reveal your home address, workplace, or travel history.
    • Security: Device make, model, and serial numbers in EXIF metadata can help fingerprint your device.
    • File size: Removing EXIF data marginally reduces file size, which can matter for large batches.

    JPEG vs. PNG stripping

    • JPEG: EXIF is removed via a binary splice — the image pixels are never re-encoded, so there is zero quality loss.
    • PNG: Metadata is removed by redrawing the image onto a canvas and re-exporting. PNG is lossless, so visual quality is unchanged, though file size may vary slightly.
